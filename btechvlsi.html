BTech Programme in Electronics and VLSI Engineering

ECE Department is launching BTech Programme in Electronics and VLSI Engineering

Number of seats: 30

INFORMATION

Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering is currently offering BTech programme in Electronics and Communication Engineering as well as MTech (VLSI Design). Considering the substantial demand for the skilled manpower in the domain of VLSI, the department is launching BTech programme in Electronics and VLSI Engineering with an initial intake of 30. The vision of this undergraduate course of B.Tech in Electronics and VLSI Engineering is to create a pool of semiconductors engineers and skilled manpower. As per the Government of India mandate and vision, India will be hub of semiconductor technology, creating lot of opportunities for aspirants skilled in VLSI and related areas.

OPPURTUNITIES

The VLSI engineering field is highly in demand for the semiconductor industries that are incorporating hard to bring artificial intelligence and machine learning chip for accelerating in the computing world. The major motivation for becoming a VLSI engineer is the digitalized world which includes several electronic chips and devices. Every electronic and technical device has an integrated circuit that is utilized as basic gates, amplifiers, microprocessors, memories, etc., which are high in demand, increasing the scope for VLSI engineers. There are numerous job opportunities for VLSI engineers in the semiconductor industry. The engineer needs to know their strengths and personal attributes for choosing the right suitable job.

The Indian government has launched the National Policy on Electronics (NPE) to promote the growth of the semiconductor industry in India. As part of the NPE, the Indian government has launched the Electronics Manufacturing Cluster (EMC) scheme, which aims to create world-class infrastructure for the electronics industry. It aims to create an ecosystem for semiconductor research and development in India. This creates opportunities for researchers, scientists, and engineers who want to work on cutting-edge semiconductor technologies.

Design engineers are responsible for designing and optimizing the circuits that are used in electronic devices. This involves designing the logic gates, building blocks, and other components that make up the circuit.
Verification engineers are responsible for ensuring that the design of electronic circuits and systems meets the desired specifications and requirements. Verification engineers use simulation and testing techniques to identify and correct any errors or issues in the design before it goes into production.
AMS designers use simulation tools to test and analyze the performance of the analog and mixed-signal circuit design. This helps them identify any potential issues and optimize the design for performance, noise, linearity, and other parameters.
Research scientists in the VLSI (Very Large Scale Integration) industry are responsible for conducting research and development (R&D) activities to advance the state of the art in the design and manufacturing of electronic circuits and systems. They work on developing new technologies, tools, and methodologies that can be used to design and manufacture better and more complex electronic devices.

Programme Structure

  • The first and second semesters of the BTech Electronics and VLSI Engineering programme are common with all other BTech programmes. This allows flexibility to students in moving from one program to the other.
  • The third semester is common with the ECE programme.
  • The fourth and fifth semester of the programme are relatively fixed, comprising mostly core courses for the programme.
  • From sixth semester onward the program can be mostly flexible, consisting of electives.

Advanced VLSI Lab

The department was involved during IInd phase of SMDP project as well as SMDP-C2SD project which was initiative of MieTY, Government of India with an objective to generate skilled man power in VLSI domain and related areas. Under these projects, advanced VLSI lab has been setup which includes industry standard such as EDA tools, Mentor Graphics, Cadence etc. used for VLSI design flow. The department has also started communicating with industries such as Cadence, Qualcomm, Texas, Intel, Silicon Labs, AMD and Synopsis for collaboration in terms of industry sponsored labs, curriculum development and co-teaching activities etc
